Big Sky Tavern & Gaming

A premium bar and gaming establishment in Missoula, Montana, combining craft cocktails, local beers, and sophisticated video gaming entertainment.

Experience the spirit of Montana with a modern twist.

Executive Summary

Business Concept

Big Sky Tavern & Gaming is a premium bar and gaming establishment planned for Missoula, Montana. We aim to merge the allure of craft cocktails and a curated local beer selection with the excitement of video poker and slot machine entertainment. Our venue will cultivate a sophisticated yet welcoming atmosphere, designed to appeal to both Missoula locals and the influx of tourists seeking quality drinks and responsible gaming.

Market Opportunity

The target demographic includes local residents aged 25-65 with disposable income, tourists drawn to Montana's natural attractions (12.5 million annually), and business travelers. Montana's consistent population growth and thriving tourism sector signal a significant opportunity for a unique, high-quality gaming and hospitality venue.

Financial Projections Summary

  • Total initial investment: $1,100,000
  • Projected Year 1 revenue: $1,425,000
  • Projected Year 3 revenue: $2,100,000
  • Expected ROI: 35% by Year 3
  • Break-even point: Month 14

Business Description

Detailed Concept Description

Big Sky Tavern & Gaming will embody a modern Montana lodge aesthetic. This design will feature exposed timber, natural stone accents, and curated local artwork, creating an authentic yet contemporary ambiance. The venue is planned to accommodate 120 patrons, with a distinct, comfortable gaming area housing 20 machines. To maximize revenue potential and cater to varied schedules, operating hours will be 8 AM to 2 AM daily. Entertainment offerings will include live acoustic music on weekends, engaging trivia nights, and popular sports viewing events.

Type of Gambling/Gaming Offerings

We will feature 20 video poker/slot machines, the maximum allowed under Montana law. This dedicated gaming zone will prioritize player comfort with premium seating, and will be enhanced by progressive jackpots and a comprehensive player rewards program to encourage loyalty and repeat visits.

Additional Amenities

  • Beverages: An extensive selection of craft cocktails using premium spirits, 20 rotating taps spotlighting Montana's best breweries, and a carefully chosen wine list with a focus on regional options.
  • Food: A full bar food menu featuring high-quality dishes, with ingredients sourced from local Montana suppliers whenever possible.

Proposed Location and Facility Details

  • Location: Missoula, Montana.
  • Facility: We are targeting a 4,500-5,500 square foot space. Key criteria include high visibility, ample parking, convenient proximity to hotels and business districts, and full compliance with all regulatory requirements, such as mandated distances from schools and churches.

Legal Structure

  • Entity type: Limited Liability Company (LLC).
  • Ownership: Managing member (5%), Investor member (95%).

Market Analysis

Target Market Demographics

  • Primary Market: Local Missoula and surrounding area residents aged 25-65, characterized by disposable income and an appreciation for quality experiences.
  • Secondary Market: Tourists visiting Montana, seeking authentic local experiences and entertainment.
  • Tertiary Market: Business travelers and convention attendees looking for upscale leisure options.

Competition Analysis

  • Direct Competitors: Existing traditional Montana bars that offer gaming, and more casino-focused establishments. Many of these competitors operate with dated facilities and offer limited craft beverage selections.
  • Competitive Advantages: Big Sky Tavern & Gaming will differentiate itself through a superior ambiance, a meticulously curated craft beverage program, higher quality food offerings, and sophisticated, modern marketing strategies.

Market Size and Trends

  • Montana’s gaming market currently generates approximately $360 million annually.
  • The state has demonstrated robust growth, with a 9.6% population increase over the past decade.
  • Montana attracts a significant number of tourists, with 12.5 million visitors annually.
  • The craft beverage market is expanding, with over 80 breweries in Montana and craft beer holding an 18% market share.

Our Competitive Edge

  • A unique and inviting ambiance that stands apart from typical gaming venues.
  • An unmatched craft beverage program within the local gaming establishment scene.
  • Consistently higher food quality standards compared to competitors.
  • Advanced marketing techniques leveraging both digital platforms and effective traditional channels.

Marketing and Sales Strategy

Marketing Channels and Methods

  • Digital Marketing: Targeted Google Ads campaigns, engaging social media presence (Facebook, Instagram), email marketing to subscribers, and a dynamic website featuring real-time jackpot displays and event calendars.
  • Traditional Marketing: Strategic radio partnerships with local stations, high-visibility billboards in key traffic areas, and direct mail campaigns to qualified zip codes.

Customer Acquisition Strategy

  • Grand Opening Campaign: A dedicated budget of $25,000 for a multi-faceted launch, including exclusive soft openings for VIPs and media, attractive free play promotions, and partnerships with local media outlets for coverage.
  • Ongoing Marketing: Sustained marketing efforts post-launch, utilizing a mix of the digital and traditional channels outlined above to maintain visibility and attract new customers.

Loyalty/Rewards Program

  • Players Club: A tiered loyalty system (Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum) offering escalating benefits such as free play rewards, discounts on beverages, special birthday bonuses, and invitations to exclusive member-only events.
  • Bar Rewards: A points-based system where customers earn points for every dollar spent on food and beverages. Points will be redeemable for food, drinks, branded merchandise, or converted to free play credits.

Events and Promotions

A regular schedule of weekly events designed to draw diverse crowds and encourage repeat visits:

  • Industry Night (discounts for hospitality workers)
  • Ladies Night specials
  • Trivia competitions
  • Craft Beer Feature nights (highlighting specific breweries or styles)
  • Live Music performances (e.g., acoustic sets)
  • Sports Specials during major games

Note: All promotions will be designed and executed in strict compliance with Montana Gaming Division rules and regulations, particularly concerning free alcohol and age verification.

Operations Plan

Daily Operations Procedures

  • Opening: Cash register reconciliation and float preparation, thorough testing of all gaming machines, inventory checks for bar and kitchen, pre-shift staff briefings.
  • Shift Management: Hourly monitoring of gaming revenue, continuous beverage inventory tracking, adherence to customer service standards, and enforcement of security protocols.
  • Closing: Secure lockdown of all gaming machines, comprehensive facility security checks, end-of-day financial reconciliation, and preparation for the next day's operations.

Staffing Requirements

  • Management: General Manager, Staff/Shift Manager(s).
  • Service Staff: Approximately 10 employees, including skilled bartenders, attentive servers, and professional security personnel.
  • Total Labor Costs: Projected to be 28-30% of revenue.

Security Measures

  • Cash Handling: Strict drop procedures conducted every 4 hours, use of a secure counting room with limited access, and daily armored car deposits.
  • Surveillance: 100% camera coverage of the gaming floor, cash points, entrances, and exits, with all footage retained for a minimum of 30 days.
  • Access Control: Implementation of a key card system for restricted access to sensitive areas like offices, storage, and the cash counting room.

Technology Systems

  • Gaming Systems: State-compliant Central Monitoring System (CMS), Player Tracking System (PTS) for loyalty programs, and integrated security camera network.
  • Bar Operations: Modern Point of Sale (POS) system, robust inventory management software, staff scheduling software, and integration with accounting systems.

Supplier Relationships

Key suppliers will include:

  • Food & Beverage Distributors: Companies like Cisco for broadline supplies.
  • Local Producers: Direct sourcing from local Farmer's Markets and individual Montana farms for fresh, seasonal ingredients.
  • Craft Breweries & Distilleries: Establishing direct relationships with Montana-based beverage producers.
